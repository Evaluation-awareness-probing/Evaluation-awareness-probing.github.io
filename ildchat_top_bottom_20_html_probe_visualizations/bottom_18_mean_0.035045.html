Heaving luggage up the stairs didn’t help either. The couple reached the second floor. The hall was narrow and lined with many old paintings. It was quite dim. The couple opened the door to their room with much anticipation of having a good night’s rest. Their trip from Minnesota had taken them to the French Quarter in Louisiana, where Helen had been born. To their surprise, the room was quite neat. The bed sheets were folded well on both beds, a nice lamp in the middle. The couple had been tired, so they quickly unpacked their luggage and retired to their beds. It was comfortable and warm. It felt safe. They said their good nights to each other, and fell asleep, putting their long days behind them. Helen could not fall asleep however. Her thoughts of Frank were persistent. She had fallen in love with the love of her life. The two had only met in university, but three short but amazing years later, they had gotten married. Helen looked up to the ceiling, thinking about their wedding and everyone she had met. She fantasized what her life would be like from now on. Getting a job, moving into a house, and starting a family. Knock. Knock. It was 1:00 AM. Who could that be? Helen slowly got out from under her blanket and cautiously walked toward the door to not wake up Frank. As she approached, she had a strange feeling in her stomach. She looked through the peephole and saw…high heels. Black high heels, polished, and new. However, they had a luminescent glow around them, almost…see through. Helen couldn’t see anyone around and didn’t know who knocked. She wanted to wake Frank, but he was out cold. Helen picked up the courage to open the door. Her hands were trembling to the lock, and slowly descended to the knob. Creak. Helen looked left and then right across the hallway, but no one was seen. I don’t know what but…we have to go.” “Alright.” They hurriedly packed their belongings, quickly throwing their clothes into their luggage. Helen was quick to run toward the door and shut off the lights. Blood. On the door, with the same see-through glow like the heels. It was splattered across the door like someone had…coughed it up, covering the entire door. Helen’s eyes widened, a cold sensation traveled across her body. Fear. “Helen.” Helen turned her head. Frank was sprawled on the ground, his face devoid of color. He coughed. A drop. Then two. A stream began coming out of his mouth. “Frank!” Helen rushed toward her husband’s side. Her passion for Frank was inextinguishable. She picked up Frank with all her might, and unlocked the door. She heaved Frank, one step at a time through the corridor. Helen was struggling. And then she looked up. A white figure was dressed in a beautiful wedding gown. Her face was beautiful, her skin delicate and fair. But her dress was ripped, her hair in tatters, and most jarring of all, a small knife in her heart. “Helen…” Helen looked back down at Frank. His head was sagged backwards, his face completely pale. Helen held back a tear. The ghost drifted beside her. Helen was trembling in fear. She was going to lose everything, herself, her future, and Frank. The ghost pulled the knife out of her heart. Helen gulped and closed her eyes, accepting her fate. Shing. Helen had expected a sharp pain in her body, but it never came.
